Hearing Aids

Hearing aids are devices designed to help individuals with hearing loss by amplifying sounds, making them clearer and easier to understand. These devices come in various types, sizes, and functionalities to suit different types and degrees of hearing impairment.

Custom Shell Design

Many hearing aids are custom-made to fit the wearer's ear canal or outer ear. These custom shells ensure a comfortable and secure fit, optimizing the transmission of sound into the ear. Behind the Ear Hearing Aids

Traditional behind-the-ear hearing aids are the most flexible devices available and address the broadest range of hearing loss.

A custom-made mold is made from your ear canal. This mold fits into your ear, while the hearing aid wraps around the back of your ear. There are a wide range of BTE hearing aids available, including:

  • Receiver-in-the-Canal (RIC): Modern BTE variation with receiver placed in the ear canal
  • Open Fitting: Allows natural sound to enter while amplifying only what's needed
  • Traditional BTE: Classic design for maximum power and flexibility
